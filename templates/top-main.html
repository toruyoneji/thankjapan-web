Mount Fuji(English Name)
Japanese Name: 富士山 ← Kanji or Hiragana or Katakana
Name: Fuzisan ← Romaji
description:
"It stands at an elevation of 3,776 meters, making it the highest mountain in Japan. Straddling Shizuoka and Yamanashi prefectures, it is considered a symbol of Japan for its beautiful appearance."
history:
Mount Fuji is Japan’s highest and most iconic mountain, standing at 3,776 meters. It is a stratovolcano that last erupted in 1707-1708. Mount Fuji has been sacred in Japanese culture for centuries, inspiring Shinto and Buddhist practices, art, and literature. During the Edo period (17th-19th centuries), climbing Fuji became a popular pilgrimage. Today, Mount Fuji is a UNESCO World Heritage site, a symbol of Japan, and a major destination for climbers and tourists worldwide.
